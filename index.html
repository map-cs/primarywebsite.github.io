Project abstract

Abstract

MAP-CS (Mission Aligned Programs for Computer Science) is a research-practice collaboration focused on transforming computer science curriculum design at liberal arts institutions. The project partners faculty, departments, and institutional leaders to develop and study CS programs that are deeply integrated with institutional missions, values, and local community contexts. Through iterative design, implementation, and assessment, MAP-CS aims to build sustainable models for CS pathways that broaden participation and align with liberal arts traditions.

Funding agency: U.S. National Science Foundation
Program: NSF IUES
Project duration: Sept. 15, 2025 – Sept. 15, 2028

Project focus

MAP-CS explores how computer science can be designed and sustained as a mission-aligned program within liberal arts institutions, with attention to curriculum, pathways, and institutional change.[web:1]

Curriculum and pathways

Designing CS curricula and course sequences that reflect liberal arts values, support diverse student goals, and connect CS with other disciplines across campus.

SIGCSE and ACM connections

MAP-CS is closely connected to the broader CS education community through engagement with SIGCSE, ACM, and related working groups focused on liberal arts computing.[web:1]

  • Engagement with the SIGCSE Committee on Computing Education in Liberal Arts Colleges, including shared interests in how CS fits within liberal arts missions.
  • Contributions to SIGCSE conferences, workshops, and working groups that focus on curricular models, assessment, and institutional change in computing.
  • Alignment with ACM curricular guidance, adapted for mission-aligned liberal arts contexts.
