Project abstract

Abstract

MAP-CS (Mapping Access to Pathways in Computer Science) is a multi-institution research-practice partnership focused on broadening participation in K–12 computer science education. The project brings together researchers, school districts, and community partners to design, study, and refine evidence-based strategies that help all students access high-quality computer science learning opportunities. In collaboration with educators, MAP-CS examines local contexts, builds CS pathways across grade levels, and supports teachers with professional learning and classroom resources that center equity, inclusion, and sustainability.

Funding agency: U.S. National Science Foundation
Program: CSforAll: Research and RPPs
Project type: Research–Practice Partnership

Project focus

MAP-CS aligns with national efforts to ensure that every student has the opportunity to participate in rigorous computer science and computational thinking learning experiences across K–12.[web:2]

Supporting K–12 CS pathways

Working with districts and schools to define and strengthen multi-grade pathways that integrate computer science and computational thinking from early grades through high school.

K–12 pathways Curriculum alignment District partnerships

Teacher learning and support

Partnering with educators to co-design professional learning, instructional materials, and ongoing communities of practice that enable teachers to teach rigorous, inclusive CS courses.

Professional development Communities of practice Instructional materials

Equity-centered research

Conducting mixed-methods research to understand how local conditions, policies, and practices shape who participates in computer science and how pathways can be more equitable and sustainable.

Broadening participation Inclusive practices Sustainable change
